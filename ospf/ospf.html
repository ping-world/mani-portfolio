High-level goals

  • Design a multi-area hierarchical OSPF network with multiple ABRs and an ASBR.
  • Use different area types: normal, stub, totally stubby, NSSA, and an area with a virtual link.
  • Implement route summarization, route filtering, and redistribution.
  • Configure OSPFv2 (IPv4) and OSPFv3 (IPv6).
  • Tune and troubleshoot OSPF using advanced verification and break/fix drills.

Topology design (13 routers)
  • Area 0 (Backbone): R1 (core), R2 (ABR to Area 30), R3 (ABR to Area 10), R4 (ABR to Area 20).
  • Area 10 (Normal + transit for virtual link): R5, R6 (internal), R11 (ABR to Area 40), plus R3 as ABR (0/10).
  • Area 20 (Totally stubby): R7, R8 (internal), plus R4 as ABR (0/20).
  • Area 30 (NSSA): R9 (internal), R10 (ASBR redistributing), plus R2 as ABR (0/30).
  • Area 40 (Remote normal): R13 (internal), attached via virtual link through Area 10.
  • External domain: R12 (EIGRP/BGP only, peering with R10).
Physical layout (GNS3-friendly)
  • Core ring (p2p): R1–R2–R3–R4–R1.
  • Area 10 broadcast LAN (DR/BDR): connect R3, R5, R6, R11 to “SW-Area10” (10.10.10.0/24).
  • Area 20 chain: R4–R7–R8.
  • Area 30 chain + external: R2–R9–R10–R12.
  • Area 40 remote: R11–R13.

Tasks

Task 1 – Build OSPFv2 Area 0 (Backbone)

Goal: Establish OSPFv2 in Area 0 across the core ring and validate neighbor formation, LSDB, and route installation.

Questions to answer / document

  • What LSAs are present in Area 0 at this stage (Types 1 and 2)?
  • How does the router-id affect adjacency if you change it?
Task 2 – Area 10 + DR/BDR behavior

I will document DR/BDR election results, why they won (priority vs router-id), and the Type-2 LSA observations.

Task 3 – Area 20 totally stubby

I will document routing table reduction and default-route behavior in the stub routers.

Task 4 – Area 30 NSSA + ASBR redistribution

I will document Type-7 behavior in the NSSA and translation to Type-5 outside.

Task 5 – Virtual link to Area 40

I will document broken reachability before the virtual link and restored reachability after.
